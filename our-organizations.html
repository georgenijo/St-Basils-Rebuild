Our Organizations

Our ministries provide opportunities for spiritual growth, fellowship, and service across all ages and stages of life. From nurturing children in Orthodox faith through Sunday School to supporting adults in their spiritual journey, these organizations strengthen our church family and deepen our connection to Christ and one another.

Sunday School

Our Sunday School program is dedicated to raising our children in the faith of our Lord Jesus Christ, passing on Orthodox traditions and building strong spiritual foundations. Students learn through Bible teachings and materials from the Malankara Syrian Orthodox Sunday School Association, participating in Divine Liturgy before attending 30-45 minute classes after the divine liturgy.

Our Mission

To develop biblical understanding, help children recognize God's plan for their lives, lead them to personal commitment to Jesus, and teach them to worship through prayer and song. We provide a structured learning environment where children can experience God's love while staying connected to their Orthodox heritage and Christian values.

Youth Ministry

Our Youth Ministry nurtures the spiritual growth of teenagers and young adults, providing a supportive environment to deepen faith, build friendships, and develop leadership skills while staying connected to Orthodox heritage. Through worship, fellowship, service, and spiritual formation activities, we help youth navigate modern challenges while remaining grounded in faith.

Our Mission

To foster strong Orthodox Christian identity, provide opportunities for spiritual growth through prayer and study, build a supportive community of young believers, encourage service to church and community, and develop future church leaders.

"Let no one despise your youth, but be an example to the believers in word, in conduct, in love, in spirit, in faith, in purity." - 1 Timothy 4:12

St. Paul's Men's Fellowship

St. Paul's Men's Fellowship, established in 2002 under our Archdiocese, provides spiritual nurturing and growth opportunities for men in our church community. Recognizing that men also deserve dedicated spiritual development, this fellowship works alongside St. Mary's Women's League to strengthen family life, study the Gospel, and support those in need of social and spiritual care.

The Fellowship organizes joint activities focused on family life, raising children in church tradition, spiritual growth, and serving others. Men meet regularly for prayer, study, and planning service projects that benefit both our church and the wider community.

St. Mary's Women's League

Our women's ministry focuses on deepening spiritual lives through studying God's Word and applying biblical teachings to daily life. Members meet monthly after Divine Liturgy to read Scripture, sing devotional songs, and pray for those in need, complementing the overall spiritual life of our church.

The Samajam organizes annual Lenten retreats, fundraising activities, and various service projects, with proceeds supporting those in need. Through fellowship, prayer, and service, our women strengthen both their personal faith and our church community.

"Favor is deceitful, and beauty is vain: but the woman that feareth the Lord, shall be praised." - Proverbs 31:30
